I Want Foxy to Step on me

Treddon

Abstract

I really want Foxy to step on me. It would make my whole life if Foxy would step on me. It feels so good when she digs her vigilante stilettos into the back of my armor. I want to be a footstool for Foxy, letting her use me as she pleases. I want to be her support in court, yelling “Objection” for her and holding her precious stilettos up so she does not tire. When it rains, I want her to imprint her mud-covered footprint onto the back of my armor, marking me as one chosen by her. I would never want to wash my armor to preserve this moment for eternity. It becomes art, how the print remains on the armor, how it beautifully tapers to a point at the end, how the heel point leaves a circular mark so rough but so pleasurable. It becomes poetry, how the mark born from dirt and mud becomes something so much more. Those who do not feel the same are such hopeless men - who would not want to feel the sharp stiletto tips as sharp as Barnham’s sword digging into their back?
